The English name "Egypt"
is derived from the Ancient Greek "Aígyptos" ("Αἴγυπτος"), via Middle French "Egypte" and Latin "Aegyptus".
It is reflected in early Greek Linear B tablets as "a-ku-pi-ti-yo".
The adjective "aigýpti-"/"aigýptios" was borrowed into Coptic as "gyptios", and from there into Arabic as "qubṭī", back formed into "قبط" ("qubṭ"), whence English "Copt".
The Greek forms were borrowed from Late Egyptian (Amarna) Hikuptah "Memphis", acorruption of the earlier Egyptian name>
